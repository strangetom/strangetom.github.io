Butterscotch blondie bars

50 mins Serves 15

The addition of fudge and pecans elevate these rich blondies to a new level. Take care not to overbake to ensure a chewy, gooey middle.

Ingredients

  • 225 g unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 150 g white chocolate, broken up into pieces
  • 3 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 200 g light brown soft sugar
  • 80 g golden syrup
  • 2 pinches sea salt
  • 250 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1x 100 g bag mini Cornish fudge chunks
  • 100 g pecans, roughly chopped

Instructions

  1. Melt the
    butter225 g
    and
    white chocolate150 g
    in a large, heatproof bowl set over a pan of gently simmering water, stirring occasionally. Remove from the heat and leave to cool for 10 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 160°C/Gas 4. Grease a 20 cm x 30 cm x 5 cm deep baking tin with
    butter225 g
    and line with baking paper.
  3. Using a balloon whisk, beat the
    eggs3 large
    and
    vanilla2 tsp
    into the chocolate and
    butter225 g
    mixture, followed by the
    sugar200 g
    ,
    golden syrup80 g
    and a pinch of
    sea salt2 pinches
    . Sift over the
    flour250 g
    and
    baking powder1 tsp
    and whisk into the mixture until combined. Fold through half the
    fudge1x 100 g bag
    pieces and half the
    pecans100 g
    , until evenly distributed.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared tin, smoothing it into the corners with a spoon. Mix together the remaining
    fudge1x 100 g bag
    and
    pecans100 g
    and sprinkle over the batter followed by a generous pinch of
    sea salt2 pinches
    .
  5. Bake in the oven for 25-30 minutes until a golden crust has formed and the batter has just set but still has a gentle wobble. Cool completely in the tin, then cut into 15 even-sized pieces. These blondies are best served the following day when dense and chewy.
