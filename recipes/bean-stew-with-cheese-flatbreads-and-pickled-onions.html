BalkanBean stew with cheese flatbreads and pickled onions

Serves 4    |     Prep 20 mins     |     Cook 45 mins

Iahnie is a way of cooking with just enough liquid for the ingredients to infuse and melt together. Itâ€™s a Persian technique brought to the Balkans and north of the Danube by the Ottomans, who used it in meat dishes with beans, pulses and vegetables. In Romania, it is almost always a white bean stew without meat, although I found old recipes that used potatoes, peas and even octopus instead. I serve my iahnie with scovergi, fried cheese flatbreads with crispy edges and a gooey middle, for a more satisfying meal.
