Fish, chips and mushy peas

This takeaway classic can easily be made healthy yet remain as delicious as the original. This tasty low-fat version is high in fibre, folic acid and vitamin C.

Servings: 2

Total: 50 mins

Ingredients

  • 400 g baking potatoes
  • 2 tsp olive or rapeseed oil
  • 2 slices white bread
  • 2 white fish fillets
  • 1 tbsp plain flour, seasoned
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 140 g frozen peas with mint
  • 2 tbsp half-fat creme fraiche

Instructions

  1. Heat the oven to 180°C/Gas 6. Peel the
    potatoes400 g
    , cut into thick chips, then toss with the
    oil2 tsp
    and a little salt. Arrange them on a large non-stick baking sheet and roast for 20 minutes, turning halfway through
  2. Lightly toast the
    bread2 slices
    , then pulse briefly in a food processor to make coarse breadcrumbs. Dust the
    fish2
    in the
    flour1 tbsp
    , then dip it first into the
    egg1
    and then the breadcrumbs, and coat thoroughly. Roast the
    fish2
    with the chips for a further 20 minutes or until both are golden.
  3. Just before the
    fish2
    and chips are ready, boil the
    peas140 g
    for 3-4 minutes, then drain and mash. Stir in the
    creme fraiche2 tbsp
    and season, then serve with the
    fish2
    and chips.
