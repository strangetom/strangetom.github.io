Sun-dried tomato meatball sub

3 hr 20 mins Serves 6

This sun-dried tomato meatball sub recipe is easy to make and you're going to love the deep, savoury flavour sun-dried tomatoes add to meatballs.

Ingredients

Meatballs
  • ¼ cup sun-dried tomatoes
  • ½ cup mascarpone
  • 1 egg
  • ¾ cup Italian-seasoned breadcrumbs or panko
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian oregano
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (for frying)
  • ½ teaspoon crushed red pepper
  • ½ cup hot water
Sauce
  • 1x 28 oz can whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 yellow onion (peeled and diced)
  • 6 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1x 15 oz can of crushed tomatoes
To serve
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella (sliced thinly)
  • 6 Italian hoagie buns
  • ½ oz fresh basil leaves
  • Salt, pepper, and sugar if needed

Instructions

  1. Prepare the meatballs: Place sun-dried tomatoes in a food processor and add the hot water. Let stand for 5 minutes. Pulse til finely minced.
  2. Add mascarpone and egg to the food processor and pulse until smooth. Add panko to a large bowl and pour the mixture on top. Add oregano, garlic, and crushed red pepper. Mix to combine and let stand 5 minutes.
  3. Add beef to the bowl and season all over with salt and pepper. (At this point, fry a tiny piece, taste, and then adjust seasonings.)
  4. Form the meatball mixture into big tablespoon-sized meatballs. You should have about 18 meatballs.
  5. Heat one tablespoon of neutral oil in a wide pot. Add the meatballs in an even layer and fry for a few minutes per side until browned all over. Remove from pot. Set aside.
  6. Prepare the sauce: To the pot, add diced onion and cook for 7-10 minutes. Add minced garlic and then whole peeled tomatoes and crushed tomatoes. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, add the meatballs and then simmer for 1-2 hours.
  7. Season to taste. If your tomatoes are really acidic, add a little sugar. Mash the whole peeled tomatoes as they cook.
  8. Assemble the meatball subs: Preheat the oven to 425°F.
  9. Drizzle the inside of each bun with 1 teaspoon of extra virgin olive oil. Toast upside down on a baking sheet at for 5 minutes. Remove from the oven, add the meatballs and some extra sauce, and a few slices of mozzarella on top.
  10. Turn on the broiler and broil for 3-4 minutes until cheese is bubbly and browned in spots.
  11. To serve: Garnish each sub with a few basil leaves. Enjoy!
