Lentil shepherds pie with celeriac & butter bean mash

1 hr 10 mins Serves 4 Vegetarian

The traditional topping of mashed potato for a pie like this has a high GI, so this version uses mashed butter beans with celeriac for when you fancy a lighter option.

Ingredients

For the lentils
  • 100 g red lentils
  • 2 leeks, chopped
  • 4 sticks celery, chopped
  • 1 reduced salt vegetable stock cube
  • 150 ml red wine (or water)
  • 3 heaped tsp tomato puree
  • 1 tbsp chopped thyme
For the topping
  • 800 g celeriac, peeled and chopped as though cooking potatoes
  • 210 g can butter beans, drained and rinsed
  • 50 g light soft cheese

Instructions

  1. Boil the celeriac with the butter beans until the celeriac is tender when tested with the point of a knife. Drain and roughly mash with the soft cheese, until the cheese is well mixed but the veg are still a little chunky.
  2. Meanwhile, tip the lentils into a pan with the leeks, celery and stock cube. Pour in the red wine and 600 ml water, add the tomato puree and thyme. Bring to the boil and simmer for 20-25 minutes
     until the lentils are soft and pulpy. Check towards the end of cooking that they are not drying out and add a splash more water if they are.
  3. Heat the oven to 180°C/Gas 6. Spoon the lentils into the base of four individual pie dishes then top with the celeriac mash, smoothing it to the edge of the dishes. Bake for 35 minutes
     until bubbling and golden and serve with a green veg like broccoli.
