Lamb kheema with naan bread

  • Lamb

  • 15 mins

  • 4 servings

  • Gluten Free

You may have heard of kheema before in Indian restaurants, where it is a type of naan bread. Kheema is basically a traditional Indian dish that contains lamb mince. Gluten-free: make sure the stock cube is gluten-free and serve with rice instead of naans.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 tbsp freshly grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 500 g minced lamb
  • 2 small sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 2 cm chunks
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 mug water + lamb stock cube
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 mug defrosted frozen peas
  • 1 fresh chilli, chopped
  • 1 tsp freshly chopped mint
  • naan breads

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a wok or saucepan. Add the onion and spices and fry for 1 minute.
  2. Add the mince and fry until it is no longer pink.
  3. Add the sweet potatoes, tomatoes, water, stock and bay leaves. Bring to the boil and then turn down and simmer for 20 minutes. Stir occasionally.
  4. Add the peas, chilli and mint and heat through.
  5. Serve with naan breads.
