Spanish fennel, olive and tomato rice

1 hr 10 mins Serves 6 Vegetarian Vegan Dairy Free Gluten Free

This is great for feeding a hungry crowd and is vegan and gluten-free!

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 bulbs fennel, cut into thin wedges
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp chilli flakes (optional)
  • 300 g paella rice
  • 1 litre vegetable stock, use GF stock if required
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 x 250 g pack pomodorino or baby plum tomatoes, halved
  • 150 g pitted olives (a mix of colours and varieties, if you like)
  • 15 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  • juice of ½ lemon, plus wedges to serve

Instructions

  1. Heat the oven to 160°C/Gas 4. Heat the
    oil2 tbsp
    in a wide, shallow ovenproof casserole or large frying pan. Fry the
    onion1 large
    and
    fennel2 bulbs
    until the
    fennel2 bulbs
    is tender when pierced with a knife, about 10-15 minutes. Add a splash of water if the
    onion1 large
    starts to catch. Add the
    garlic3 cloves
    and spices and fry for another minute.
  2. Add in the
    rice300 g
    , and fry for a few minutes, mixing with the veg. Pour over the stock, add the
    bay leaves2
    and season well, as the
    rice300 g
    will absorb quite a bit of salt. Bring to a simmer, then stir in the
    tomatoes1x 250 g pack
    and olives, and transfer to the oven, uncovered.
  3. Bake for 30-35 minutes until the
    rice300 g
    is tender and the stock has been absorbed. Cover with a lid or baking sheet and leave to steam for 10 minutes. Scatter with the parsley and squeeze over the lemon before serving with more lemon wedges.
