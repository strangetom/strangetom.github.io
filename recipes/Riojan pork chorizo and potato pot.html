Riojan pork, chorizo and potato pot

1 hr 10 mins Serves 4-6 Dairy Free

This typically Spanish one pot is packed with flavour and can be made-ahead - perfect for relaxed entertaining or a family midweek meal served with crusty bread.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 700 g single pork ribs
  • 250 g mini cooking chorizo, cut into 1cm discs
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • ½ medium leek, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed with the flat of a knife
  • ½ green pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 large plum tomatoes, coarsely grated
  • 100 ml white wine
  • 800 g white potatoes, peeled and chopped into 2-3 cm cubes
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 dried choricero peppers, halved, stems and seeds removed
  • 150 g baby button chestnut mushrooms
  • a few sprigs thyme, to garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the
    oil2 tbsp
    in a large, wide, deep pan or casserole. Fry the
    ribs700 g
    until browned on all sides, in batches if needed, then lift out and keep to one side. Pour off any fat from the pan. Add the
    chorizo250 g
    pieces to the pan and fry for a few more minutes until golden, then scoop out and put aside.
  2. Put the
    onions2 medium
    and
    leek½ medium
    into the pan and cook gently for about 5 minutes until softened and golden, then add the
    garlic4 cloves
    and the
    green pepper½
    and continue to cook gently for another 5 minutes. Add the grated
    tomatoes2 large
    , with any juice, and simmer for another 10 minutes.
  3. Pour in the
    wine100 ml
    and bubble up to burn off the alcohol. Then turn down the heat and simmer uncovered for another 5 minutes. Add the
    potatoes800 g
    and stir to ensure they are well coated, then return the
    ribs700 g
    to the pan, stir briefly, and add the
    paprika2 tsp
    , 800 ml water and the peppers, and bring to a simmer. Lower the heat and cook gently for about 25 minutes. Once the
    potatoes800 g
    are tender, add the
    mushrooms150 g
    and
    chorizo250 g
    and continue to cook for another 15 minutes.
  4. Serve each plate with a half of choricero pepper on top if you’re using them. The idea is to scoop out the flesh and mix it into the stew. Garnish with the
    thymea few sprigs
    to serve.
