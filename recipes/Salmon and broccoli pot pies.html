Salmon and broccoli pot pies

1 hr 5 mins Serves 4

These mouthwatering pies are quick and simple to make, but oh-so-delicious.

Ingredients

  • 2 leeks, trimmed and washed well to remove any grit
  • 25 g butter
  • 200 g Tenderstem broccoli, trimmed
  • 100 g frozen peas
  • 4 tbsp plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 400 ml whole milk
  • 4 skinless Scottish salmon fillets, cut into 3 cm chunks
  • 4 tbsp light creme fraiche
  • finely grated zest of 0.5 lemon
  • 1x 320 g Jus-Rol puff pastry sheet
  • 1 egg, beaten

Instructions

  1. Thinly slice the
    leeks2
    . Melt the
    butter25 g
    in a large pan over a medium heat, add the
    leeks2
    and cook, stirring, for 2 minutes. Cover and cook over a low heat for a further 5 minutes or until tender. While the
    leeks2
    are cooking, cut the
    broccoli200 g
    unto 5 cm lengths and cooking in boiling salted water for 3 minutes or until just tender. Stir in the
    peas100 g
    and cook for 1 minute, then drain and set aside.
  2. Stir the
    flour4 tbsp
    into the
    leeks2
    and cook, stirring, for 1 minute. Remove from the heat and stir in the
    milk400 ml
    , then return to the heat and bring to the boil, stirring all the time, until the sauce boils and thickens. Reduce the heat and cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add the
    salmon4
    to the sauce, along with the
    broccoli200 g
    and
    peas100 g
    . Stir in the
    creme fraiche4 tbsp
    and lemon zest, then season to taste. Divide the mixture between 4 x 300 ml oval pie dishes. Set aside to cool slightly.
  4. Preheat the oven to 200°C/Gas 7. Gently unroll the pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface and use a knife to mark a cross hatch pattern on the pastry. Cut into 4 rectangles and 2 cm bigger than the pie dishes. Brush a little beaten
    egg1
    around the edge of each pie dish, then lay the pastry on top and press the edges to seal. Make a small cut in the centre of each pie to allow the steam to escape, then brush the pastry lid all over with the beaten
    egg1
    .
  5. Put the pies on a baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes or until the pastry is puffed up and golden.
