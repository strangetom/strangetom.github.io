Spanish lamb with sherry, honey & peppers

Open a bottle of sherry for this hearty lamb casserole, rich with sweet and savoury flavours. However, if you don't have saffron, just leave it out.

165 mins
4 servings

  • kcal 507
  • fat 32 g
  • saturates 14 g
  • carbs 12 g
  • sugar 11 g
  • fibre 3 g
  • protein 32 g
  • salt 0.3 g

Ingredients

  • 1 kg lamb shoulder, trimmed of excess fat, then cubed
  • 1.5 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, roughly chopped
  • 1 red pepper, halved, deseeded and sliced
  • 1 yellow pepper, halved, deseeded and sliced
  • 1 green pepper, halved, deseeded and sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, grated to a purée
  • 0.5 tsp paprika
  • 250 ml medium sherry
  • 250 ml lamb stock
  • generous pinch of saffron threads
  • 1 tbsp sherry vinegar
  • 1.5 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped parsley, or chopped toasted blanched almonds
  • cooked couscous, to serve

Instructions

  1. Make sure your lamb isn’t too damp – pat it dry with kitchen paper if you need to, otherwise it won’t colour well. Heat 1 tbsp
    olive oil1.5 tbsp
    in a large casserole dish and brown the lamb over a high heat in batches (if you crowd the pan the meat will sweat and not brown). Remove with a slotted spoon as you go. When all the lamb is browned, add the remaining oil and cook the
    onion1 large
    and peppers over a medium heat until the
    onion1 large
    is golden and the vegetables are softened. Add the garlic and
    paprika0.5 tsp
    , and cook for a further 1 min, then add the sherry and bring to a simmer.
  2. Put all the lamb back in the pan, along with the stock, saffron,
    sherry vinegar1 tbsp
    and
    honey1.5 tbsp
    . Season and bring to just under the boil. Turn the heat down, cover the pan and leave to cook very gently for 2 hrs, turning the meat every so often.
  3. In the last half hour of cooking time, remove the lid so that the sherry and stock can reduce. You want to end up with completely tender meat in a flavoursome gravy – how long this takes will depend on how wide your pan is so you may need to give it longer than 30 mins. Check the seasoning. You’re unlikely to need to adjust the vinegar or
    honey1.5 tbsp
    but you might want to sweeten the dish slightly. Scatter the parsley or almonds on top (or use both). Serve with the couscous.
