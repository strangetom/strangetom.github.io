Caribbean fish pie

40 mins
4-5 servings

Ingredients

  • 600 ml milk
  • 1 sprig thyme
  • 600 g skinned white fish fillets
  • 55 g butter
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 50 g block creamed coconut, roughly chopped
  • salt and pepper
  • 1.5 limes, juiced
  • leaves from a large bunch coriander, chopped
  • 250 g young leaf spinach, washed
  • 900 g sweet potatoes, peeled
  • 40 g butter
  • good grating of nutmeg
  • pinch of ground allspice
  • salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180/Gas 4.
  2. To make the mash, cut the potatoes into medium equal sized chunks. Put in cold water, bring to the boil and simmer until tender, around 8-10 minutes. Mash with the
    butter40 g
    and season with nutmeg, allspice, salt and pepper, to taste.
  3. Warm the
    milk600 ml
    and
    thyme1 sprig
    in a saucepan large enough to hold the ﬁsh until it reaches boiling point. Remove from the heat and leave to one side to infuse for about 30 minutes.
  4. Put the ﬁsh in the infused
    milk600 ml
    . Bring to the boil, turn off the heat and leave to stand for 5 minutes. Remove the ﬁsh (reserve the
    milk600 ml
    ) and divide into large ﬂakes. Discard the
    thyme1 sprig
    .
  5. Make the sauce by melting half the
    butter40 g
    in a large saucepan and adding the flour. Cook, stirring, for a couple of minutes over a low heat. Gradually add the warm
    milk600 ml
    from cooking the fish, whisking all the time to avoid lumps. When it thickens, stir in the creamed coconut until it melts into the sauce. Season with salt, pepper and lime juice to taste - some may like a stronger taste of citrus in the sauce than others. Add the coriander, if using. Stir in the spinach so it just wilts in the heat of the sauce. Stir in the fish very gently so it stays in the largest possible flakes. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  6. Put the fish mixture in an ovenproof dish about as big as an A4 piece of paper. Top with the Caribbean Mash and dot with the remaining
    butter40 g
    . Put on a baking sheet (this will save you cleaning the oven in case of spillages) and cook in the preheated oven for 20-30 minutes until bubbling hot.
