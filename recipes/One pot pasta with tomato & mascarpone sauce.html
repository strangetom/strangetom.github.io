One pot pasta with tomato & mascarpone sauce

A quick and easy one pot pasta packed with nutritious greens and a thick, luxurious tomato & mascarpone sauce. Excellent midweek dinner and perfect for Meatless Monday.

30 mins
5 servings

  • kcal 582
  • fat 15.1 g
  • saturates 6.3 g
  • carbs 87.4 g
  • sugar 3.9 g
  • fibre 7.1 g
  • protein 27.6 g
  • salt 1.06 g

Ingredients

  • 500 g fusilli
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium red onion or 0.5 of a large one, chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tbsp dried Italian herbs
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2x 400 g cans chopped tomatoes with liquid
  • 140 g tomato paste/puree, you may want more or less depending on how watery your canned tomatoes are so use your judgement here
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 950 ml boiling water (you may need more)
  • a few handfuls fresh basil
  • 250 g fresh spinach (try adding some rocket too!)
  • 25-50 g butter
  • 3 heaping spoonfuls mascarpone

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat some
    olive oil1 tbsp
    and gently saute your
    onions1 medium
    until soft.
  2. Add the
    garlic5 cloves
    and
    herbs1 tbsp
    , and saute on a very low heat for a further 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add
    tomatoes2x 400 g cans
    ,
    tomato paste140 g
    ,
    salt2 tsp
    and
    balsamic vinegar1 tbsp
    . Cook for 5 minutes, until bubbling.
  4. Now add the fusilli along with the
    boiling water950 ml
    . The sauce will be very watery and runny but don't worry- the
    pasta500 g
    will absorb all the excess water as it cooks.
  5. Bring to the boil, and then simmer for 10 to 12 minutes, stirring often, until the
    pasta500 g
    has cooked and the sauce has thickened back up. If it goes too dry, add some more water. It's hard to say how much you'll need because some
    pasta500 g
    brands are thicker than others.
  6. Now add the
    spinach250 g
    ,
    basila few handfuls
    and
    butter25-50 g
    , and stir over a low heat until it's cooked down.
  7. Finally, stir in the
    mascarpone3 heaping spoonfuls
    until melted. You should have a deliciously thick, creamy sauce.
