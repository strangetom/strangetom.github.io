Pan fried chicken with lime & coconut sauce

  • Chicken

  • 30 mins

  • 4 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 mugs basmati rice
  • 1 tsp pillau rice seasoning
  • 2 tbsp freshly chopped coriander
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 3 chicken breasts
  • 3 chicken breasts
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 2 tbsp freshly grated ginger
  • juice of a lime
  • 1 chicken stick cube
  • 2 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 400 ml can coconut milk
  • 400 ml can coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 4 pak choi, sliced

Instructions

  1. Put the rice on the cook in 3 mugs of boiling water. Add the pilau rice seasoning and simmer for 12 minutes. Just before you serve the rice, add the chopped coriander.
  2. Heat the oil in a frying pan and add the chicken breasts. Season well. Cook on a fairly high heat for 2 minutes each side. Turn down to medium heat and cook for a further 4 minutes each side, with a lid on the pan. If the chicken breasts are smaller, then cook for less time. Remove from the pan and set to one side until needed.
  3. To make the sauce, heat the toasted sesame oil in a small saucepan and gently fry the garlic and ginger. Add the rest of the sauce ingredients and simmer gently for 5-6 minutes.
  4. Heat the other tablespoon of toasted sesame oil in the frying pan and stir-fry the pak choi for 1 minutes, or until it begins to wilt slights; it needs to be quite 'al dente'.
