Fish pie with saffron mash

A great dinner party dish. This can be made ahead and frozen so there's always something ready to entertain guests.

Servings: 6

Total: 1 hr 45 mins

Ingredients

  • 3 shallots, finely chopped
  • 3 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 150 ml white wine
  • 142 ml pot double cream
  • 900 g fresh haddock fillets, skinned
  • 200 g bag raw peeled prawns, preferably unfrozen
  • 800 g fresh spinach (about 2 bags)
  • 200 g crabmeat
For the topping
  • 3 clove garlic
  • 1 red chilli, halved and deseeded
  • 200 ml full-fat milk
  • pinch saffron strands
  • 1250 g potato, cut into large chunks
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • zest and juice 1 lemon

Instructions

    shallots3
    and

    into a saucepan and place on the heat so the

    evaporates quickly. Splash in the
    white wine150 ml
    and reduce until practically dry. Add
    cream142 ml
    , bring to the boil, then simmer gently until reduced by two-thirds and very thick. Leave to cool. Cut the
    fish900 g
    into bite-size chunks and set aside with the
    prawns200 g bag
    .
  2. Tip
    spinach800 g
    into a large colander and pour over boiling water from a kettle until all the
    spinach800 g
    is wilted (you may need to do this more than once). Cool under the cold tap, then squeeze the
    spinach800 g
    tightly to get rid of any excess liquid.
  3. To make the topping, mash the saffron,
    garlic3 clove
    and
    chilli1
    together using a pestle and mortar. Tip into a saucepan with the
    milk200 ml
    , bring to the boil, then remove from the heat to infuse. Bring the potatoes to the boil and simmer for 15 mins until soft. Drain, then mash well with the infused
    milk200 ml
    ,
    olive oil4 tbsp
    , lemon juice and zest.
  4. Heat oven to 200°C/fan 180°C/Gas 6. To assemble the pie, mix the
    fish900 g
    and
    prawns200 g bag
    into the cold
    cream142 ml
    until completely coated. Tip the
    fish900 g
    into the bottom of a large gratin dish and scatter over clumps of
    crabmeat200 g
    . Unravel the
    spinach800 g
    and lay it over the
    fish900 g
    , then top with the mash. Cook the pie for about 30 mins until golden and starting to brown and bubble around the edges. Serve with green veg if you like.
