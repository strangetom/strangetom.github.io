One-pan lamb & couscous

25 mins Serves 2 British

Whip up a deliciously flavoursome lamb dish in one pot and within half an hour.

Ingredients

  • 2 lamb steaks, approx 140 g each or 4 lamb chops trimmed of excess fat
  • 1-2 tsp harissa paste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • handful dried fruit and nuts
  • 85 g couscous
  • 1x 400 g can chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 100 ml hot chicken stock or lamb stock (from a cube is fine)
  • handful mint leaves, torn

Instructions

  1. Rub the lamb with half the harissa paste. Heat the oil in a frying pan and fry the lamb for 3 mins
     on each side for medium-rare, or a little longer for well-done. Lift the meat out of the pan and tip in the remaining harissa, the dried fruit and nuts, couscous and chickpeas, then stir to coat in the paste.
  2. Take the pan off the heat and pour in the stock. Stir briefly, return the lamb to the pan and cover with a lid or tightly-fitting foil for 10 mins
     until the couscous has absorbed all the liquid.
  3. Fluff up the couscous with a fork, season to taste and fold the mint leaves through to serve.
