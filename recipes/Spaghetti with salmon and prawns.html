Spaghetti with salmon and prawns

This is a lovely fresh-tasting pasta dish, perfect for an al fresco meal in summer. Serve it as a main course lunch with warm ciabatta or focaccia and a dry white wine.

30 mins
4 servings

  • kcal 701
  • fat 30.6 g
  • saturates 14.3 g
  • carbs 70.4 g
  • sugar 8.5 g
  • fibre 4 g
  • protein 32.4 g

Ingredients

  • 300 g salmon fillet
  • 200 ml dry white wine
  • a few sprigs fresh basil, plus extra basil leaves to garnish
  • 150 ml double cream
  • 6 ripe Italian plum tomatoes, peeled and finely chopped
  • 350 g dried spaghetti
  • 115 g prawns, peeled and cooked
  • salt and ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Put the
    salmon300 g
    skin side up in a wide shallow pan. Pour the
    wine200 ml
    over, then add the
    basila few sprigs
    sprigs to the pan and sprinkle the fish with salt and pepper. Bring the
    wine200 ml
    to the boil, cover the pan and simmer gently for no more than 5 minutes. Using a fish slice, lift the fish out of the pan and set it aside to cool a little.
  2. Add the
    cream150 ml
    and
    tomatoes6
    to the liquid remaining in the pan and bring to the boil. Stir well, then lower the heat and simmer, uncovered, for 10-15 minutes. Meanwhile, cook the
    pasta350 g
    in a large pan of lightly salted boiling water for 10-12 minutes.
  3. Flake the fish into large chunks, discarding the skin and any bones. Add the fish the sauce with the
    prawns115 g
    , shaking he pan until the fish and shellfish and well coated. Taste the sauce for seasoning.
  4. Drain the
    pasta350 g
    and tip it into a warm bowl. Pour the sauce over the
    pasta350 g
    and toss to combine. Serve immediately, garnished with the
    basila few sprigs
    .
