Sea-fisherman's pie

55 mins Serves 4

If you don't have a day to prepare, just buy some all butter puff pastry from the supermarket.

Ingredients

For the puff pastry
  • 225 g strong white bread flour, plus more to dust
  • Â½ tsp salt
  • 225 g unsalted butter, chilled
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
For the filling
  • 4 spring onions, chopped
  • olive oil
  • 30 g unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 250 ml whole milk
  • 50 ml dry white wine
  • 75 g Cheddar cheese, grated
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 25 g chives, chopped
  • 75 g frozen peas
  • 75 g frozen sweetcorn
  • 300 g chopped mixed fish
  • 100 g raw shelled prawns
  • sea salt and black pepper

Instructions

  1. To make the puff pastry, first mix the the
    bread flour225 g
    and
    salt½ tsp
    in a large bowl, then chop 25 g of the
    butter30 g
    and rub into the flour with your fingers. Add the
    lemon juice1 tsp
    and 140 m of cold water and mix with a knife until the mixture is brought together enough to handle. Tip out on to a floured work surface and knead until smooth and elastic (around 5-10 minutes). Allow the dough to rest for 5 minutes.
  2. Roll out on a floured surface to rectangle of 51 x 17 cm (or thereabouts). Roll out the remaining 200 g
    butter30 g
    between sheets of cling film to a rectangle of 33 x 15 cm. You'll need to peel off and reapply the cling film regularly to stop it ripping. Working quickly now, peel the top layer of cling film off the
    butter30 g
    and flip the
    butter30 g
    on to the rolled out dough, covering two-thirds of the length of the dough and also leaving a clear rim around all the edges. Fold the unbuttered dough on to half of the
    butter30 g
    and press the edges to seal the
    butter30 g
    in. Fold this layer over the remaining
    butter30 g
    and press down the edges all around to form a 17 cm square. Wrap in cling film and chill in the fridge for 30 minutes.
  3. Take out of the fridge and roll back out to about 51 x 17 cm. Fold up in thirds as before, to make a square. Turn the square through 90 degrees and roll out to 51 x 17 cm again, then fold up as before. Wrap in cling film and chill for another 30 minutes.
  4. Repeat the last step, so the pastry has been folded a total of 5 times. Chill for another 30 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, make the filling. Fry the
    spring onions4
    in the olive oil in a large pan over a medium heat for 2-3 minutes. Set aside. Melt the
    butter30 g
    in the same pan and stir in the
    plain flour2 tbsp
    to form a thick paste. Gradually add the
    milk250 ml
    , about 50 ml at a time, whisking well between each addition. (If you add it all at once, you'll have a lumpy sauce.) Whisk in the
    wine50 ml
    , then stir in the
    cheese75 g
    ,
    mustard1 tsp
    and
    garlic2 cloves
    . Allow to cook for a couple of minutes, remembering to make sauce the sauce doesn't dry on the sides of the pan. Add the
    spring onions4
    , chopped
    chives25 g
    ,
    peas75 g
    and
    sweetcorn75 g
    and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Add the
    fish300 g
    and
    prawns100 g
    and cook for another 5 minutes, then season with
    salt½ tsp
    and pepper and scrape the filling into a 23 x 18 cm rectangular deep sided pie dish. Leave to cool
  6. Preheat the oven to 220°C/Gas 9. Roll out the puff pastry on a floured work surface to a square roughly 30 cm and cut off enough 3 cm wide strips to lay around the rim of the dish. Use plenty of beaten
    egg1 large
    to stick these strips around the edge, then brush their tops with more
    egg1 large
    . Roll up the remaining pastry on to a rolling pin and lay on top of the pie dish, pressing the top layer of puff down on to the strips, then cut off the excess.
  7. Gently use a sharp knife to cut small grooves along the side edges of the pastry to expose some of the puff layers. This allows the pastry to puff up more easily when baking.
  8. Paint with beaten
    egg1 large
    and bake in the oven for 20-25 minutes until golden brown. Serve piping hot with new potatoes and green beans.
