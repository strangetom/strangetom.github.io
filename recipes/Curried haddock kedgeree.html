Curried haddock kedgeree

A classic kedgeree designed to fill you with warmth and spice. This gluten-free family meal is an easy midweek fix.

Servings: 4

Total: 25 mins

Gluten Free

Ingredients

  • 500 ml double cream
  • thumb-sized piece ginger, peeled and grated
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 400 g undyed smoked haddock fillets (skin on)
  • 300 g cooked basmati rice
  • 200 g frozen peas
  • 2 large eggs, boiled for 7 mins, peeled and cut into quarters
  • 1 small pack coriander, leaves roughly chopped

Instructions

  1. Pour the
    cream500 ml
    into a large sauté pan with a lid. Stir in the
    gingerthumb-sized piece
    and spices, then submerge the
    smoked haddock fillets400 g
    , skin-side up. Put the lid on, bring the
    cream500 ml
    slowly to the boil, then remove from the heat and leave to cool. Flake the fish into a bowl in large pieces, discarding the skin.
  2. Put the spiced
    cream500 ml
    back on a medium heat. Once warm, add the cooked
    rice300 g
    and
    peas200 g
    and stir through to combine. Cook for 3 mins until everything is heated through.
  3. Gently mix in the haddock, being careful to keep it in large flakes. Cook for a further 2 mins and check the seasoning - the smoked haddock will be quite salty, but a good grind of black pepper could be welcome.
  4. Top the kedgeree with the boiled
    eggs2 large
    and scatter over the
    coriander1 small pack
    , then tip it into a large dish and take it to the table to serve.
