Summer tagliatelle ragu

This thrifty summer-inspired ragu is perfect for a quick supper in the garden

Servings: 4

Total: 35 mins

Ingredients

  • 1x 500 g pack beef mince
  • 1x 30 g pack basil
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 200 g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 medium carrots, grated coarsely
  • 2 medium courgettes, grated coarsely
  • 1 beef stock cube
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • , grated zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 400 g dried tagliatelle
  • 40 g finely grated Italian hard cheese

Instructions

  1. Fry the
    beef mince1x 500 g pack
    in a large deep frying pan or casserole (no need for any oil), for about 8 minutes over a high heat until well browned, stirring to break it up as it cooks.
  2. Take the
    basil1x 30 g pack
    leaves off the stalks and put aside. Finely chop the stalks and add to the mince with the
    garlic2 cloves
    and
    mushrooms200 g
    . Cook, stirring, for 2 minutes, then mix in the
    carrots2 medium
    and
    courgettes2 medium
    and cook for a further 2 minutes. Crumble in the
    stock cube1
    and stir in the
    flour2 tbsp
    , cook for 1 minute then add half each of the lemon zest and juice plus 400 ml boiling water and some seasoning.
  3. Simmer, uncovered, for 10-15 minutes, adding an extra splash of water if the sauce starts looking dry at any point, although it shouldn't be swimming in liquid.
  4. Meanwhile, add the
    tagliatelle400 g
    to a large pan of salted boiling water and cook for 9-10 minutes until al dente, stirring to separate the noodles.
  5. Roughly shred most of the
    basil1x 30 g pack
    leaves, keeping the small leaves to garnish. Mix into the beef ragu with the rest of the lemon juice and half the
    cheese40 g
    . Drain the
    tagliatelle400 g
    and toss together with the sauce. Divide between bowls and scatter with the rest of the
    cheese40 g
    , lemon zest and
    basil1x 30 g pack
    leaves to serve.
