Lentil shepherds pie with celeriac & butter bean mash

The traditional topping of mashed potato for a pie like this has a high GI, so this version uses mashed butter beans with celeriac for when you fancy a lighter option.

Servings: 4

Total: 1 hr 10 mins

Vegetarian

Ingredients

For the lentils
  • 100 g red lentils
  • 2 leeks, chopped
  • 4 sticks celery, chopped
  • 1 reduced salt vegetable stock cube
  • 150 ml red wine (or water)
  • 3 heaped tsp tomato puree
  • 1 tbsp chopped thyme
For the topping
  • 800 g celeriac, peeled and chopped as though cooking potatoes
  • 210 g can butter beans, drained and rinsed
  • 50 g light soft cheese

Instructions

  1. Boil the
    celeriac800 g
    with the
    butter beans210 g can
    until the
    celeriac800 g
    is tender when tested with the point of a knife. Drain and roughly mash with the
    soft cheese50 g
    , until the cheese is well mixed but the veg are still a little chunky.
  2. Meanwhile, tip the lentils into a pan with the
    leeks2
    ,
    celery4 sticks
    and
    stock cube1
    . Pour in the
    red wine150 ml
    and 600 ml water, add the
    tomato puree3 heaped tsp
    and
    thyme1 tbsp
    . Bring to the boil and simmer for 20-25 minutes until the lentils are soft and pulpy. Check towards the end of cooking that they are not drying out and add a splash more water if they are.
  3. Heat the oven to 180°C/Gas 6. Spoon the lentils into the base of four individual pie dishes then top with the
    celeriac800 g
    mash, smoothing it to the edge of the dishes. Bake for 35 minutes until bubbling and golden and serve with a green veg like broccoli.
