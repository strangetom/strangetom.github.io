South indian-style coconut fish curry

Done in about 20 minutes. It's an absolute winner. The lemon works a treat here, bringing the freshness. The perfect mid-week dinner.

20 mins
4 servings

Ingredients

  • ground turmeric
  • ground cumin
  • coriander seeds
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • fresh ginger
  • 550 g frozen white fish fillets
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 brown onion
  • 4 vine tomatoes
  • 1 red chilli
  • 400 g basmati rice
  • 1x 400 ml tin coconut milk
  • fresh coriander
  • olive oil
  • salt

  1. Prep your spice paste. Add 1 tsp of turmeric, 1 tsp of cumin, 2.5 tsp of coriander seeds,
    garlic3 cloves
    and a peeled 5 cm piece of ginger into a blender. Blitz until you have a rough paste
  2. Defrost the
    white fish fillets550 g
    then place in a bowl. Grate in the zest and juice of 0.5
    lemon1
    and add a small pinch of turmeric. Mix it all together and cover.
  3. Finely chop a brown
    onion1
    . Fry with a splash of oil in a wide frying pan until soft, then add your spice paste. You want in to be quite dry in the pan - don't be tempted to add more oil. Once the spices start getting toasty, grate the vine
    tomatoes4
    , discarding the skins, and add them to the pan. Grate the
    chilli1
    into the pan and mix everything together.
  4. Get the
    rice400 g
    on (following the instructions on the packet)
  5. Once the moisture from the
    tomatoes4
    has evaporated, add the
    coconut milk1x 400 ml tin
    . Stir everything together, and then add the fish, in large bite-sized chunks. Season the curry with salt. The fish should take about 5 minutes to cook perfectly.
  6. Once the curry has thickened, throw in a handful of fresh coriander, squeeze in a bit more
    lemon1
    juice, stir it in and remove from the heat.
  7. Serve on top of the hot
    rice400 g
