Beef & ale slice pie

Servings: 4

Total: 4 hr 0 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 onion
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 sticks celery
  • 800 g stewing/braising steak
  • 2 heaped tsp plain flour
  • 500 ml bottle dark ale
  • 500 ml beef stock
  • few sprigs thyme
  • 2 tbsp brown sauce
  • 2 packs ready rolled shortcrust pastry
  • 1½ tbsp english mustard
  • 1 beaten egg

Instructions

  1. Chop the
    onion1
    , carrot and
    celery2 sticks
    into the same sized pieces. Season the
    steak800 g
    generously with salt and pepper and toss in the
    plain flour2 heaped tsp
    so that each piece is evenly coated.
  2. Pour a splash of oil into a large saucepan/casserole dish. Over a high heat, fry the
    steak800 g
    in batches until evenly browned. Remove onto a plate.
  3. Turn down the heat, don’t worry about the pan bottom being brown this is all flavour! Tip in the
    onion1
    , carrot and
    celery2 sticks
    into the pan. Cook, stirring for 5 minutes until softened then pour in the
    ale500 ml bottle
    and
    stock500 ml
    . Stir in the
    thymefew sprigs
    and
    brown sauce2 tbsp
    . Scrape the meat back into the pan, Simmer gently on a low heat, uncovered for 2.5 hours until you have a very thick pie filling. If the gravy is too thin, remove the meat then bubble the sauce down. Leave to cool - you can do this day before if you want.
  4. Preheat the oven to 180°C fan.
  5. Pie time. Unravel your first sheet of shortcrust
    pastry2 packs
    with its paper onto a baking tray. Spread with the
    mustard1½ tbsp
    then spoon in your filling keeping a 2 cm border free around the edge. Smooth the filling over then brush the border with the
    beaten egg1
    . Place your second shortcrust
    pastry2 packs
    sheet on top (roll it out first to make sure it fits with enough overlap.
  6. Press the two sheets of
    pastry2 packs
    together around the edge, cut off any messy bits then press down along all the edges with a fork to create a seal.
  7. Score the top with a cutlery knife. Don’t press the knife in too far as you don’t want to cut the
    pastry2 packs
    open. Bake in the oven for 30 mins until deep golden brown.
  8. Slice and serve with a dollop of
    brown sauce2 tbsp
    , or
    mustard1½ tbsp
    , or both if you are a maverick.
