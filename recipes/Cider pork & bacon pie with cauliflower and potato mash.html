Cider pork & bacon pie with cauliflower and potato mash

This is a good, everyday pie, using pork mince, which is slightly less expensive than lamb or beef. Gluten-free: use gluten-free plain flour in the sauce.

25 mins
4 servings

Ingredients

  • 5 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into 2 cm chunks
  • 1 small cauliflower, cut into small pieces
  • 3/4 mug grated Cheddar cheese
  • 1 tsp wholegrain mustard
  • Filling
  • 150 g streaky bacon
  • 1 onion
  • 500 g pork mince
  • 2 leeks, sliced
  • 1 tbsp plain flour
  • 1 mug cider
  • 1 veg stock cube

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C/Gas 7. Grease a large casserole dish.
  2. Put the
    potatoes5 medium
    and the
    cauliflower1 small
    in a saucepan of boiling water. Bring to the boil and then turn down to simmer for 10 minutes. Drain and return to the ban. Add the
    cheese3/4 mug
    and
    mustard1 tsp
    and mash together.
  3. Meanwhile, fry the
    bacon150 g
    in a large frying pan until it is crispy. Remove from the pan and cut into small pieces.
  4. In the same frying pan, fry the onions until they become soft. Add the
    mince500 g
    and fry until it is no longer pink.
  5. Add the
    leeks2
    and fry for 2 minutes. Add the
    flour1 tbsp
    and stir well.
  6. Add the
    cider1 mug
    and the
    stock cube1
    . Mix together, bring to the boil and season. Add the chopped
    bacon150 g
    and pour into the casserole dish.
  7. Put the mash on top. Place in the oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is browned.
