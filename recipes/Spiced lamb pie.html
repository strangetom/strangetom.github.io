Spiced lamb pie

This stunning filo pastry pie is a twist on a lamb tagine. A hearty main meal that wouldn't look out of place on any dinner-party table

290 mins
4 servings

  • kcal 595
  • fat 30 g
  • saturates 10 g
  • carbs 45 g
  • sugar 11 g
  • fibre 4 g
  • protein 34 g
  • salt 0.8 g

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 800 g lamb shoulder, fat removed diced
  • 3 tbsp plain flour
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tbsp ras el hanout
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • good pinch of ground cloves
  • good pinch of saffron (optional)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 400 g can , chopped tomatoes
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 4 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 270 g pack filo pastry (4-6 sheets)
  • 50 g butter, melted
  • 50 g flaked almonds
  • 50 g pomegranate seeds

Instructions

  1. Heat the
    oil3 tbsp
    in a large flameproof casserole dish. Toss the
    lamb800 g
    in the
    flour3 tbsp
    with some seasoning, then brown it in the dish. Do this in batches so you don't overcrowd the dish (which will cause the meat to steam instead of fry). Take your time to build up a deep brown colour, which will add lots of meaty flavour to the stew. Set the meat aside on a plate as you go.
  2. Add the remaining
    oil3 tbsp
    to the dish along with the
    onion1 large
    and
    garlic2 cloves
    . Cook for 5 mins until softened, then stir in the spices,
    tomatoes400 g can
    ,
    lemon1
    zest and juice,
    honey1 tbsp
    and
    molasses4 tbsp
    . Fill the tomato can halfway with water, swill around and add this too. Season well, bring to a simmer and cover with a lid. Reduce the heat and cook for 2 hrs 30 mins-3 hrs or until the meat is tender. Leave to cool.
  3. Heat oven to 180°C/160°C fan/Gas 4. Transfer the meat to a pie dish that's about 25 cm wide, or leave it in the casserole if it's wide enough to fit the topping. Unwrap the
    filo270 g pack
    and brush a sheet with
    butter50 g
    , scrunch up and place on top of the pie. Continue with the remaining sheets until the tagine is covered. Drizzle any remaining
    butter50 g
    over the top and scatter over the
    almonds50 g
    , then bake for 45 mins or until the pastry is crisp and golden. Scatter over the
    pomegranate seeds50 g
    just before serving.
