Ginger steamed salmon with wild rice

15 mins Serves 4

A quick, light, healthy dinner. No added fat. Perfect for post-gym or pre-night out. So easy to do.

Ingredients

  • 1 lime
  • 4 salmon fillets
  • fresh ginger
  • 1-2 red chillis
  • fresh coriander
  • soy sauce
  • 400 g wild rice

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C/Gas 6.
  2. Cut the
    lime1
    into thin slices and place them on a baking sheet. Lay the
    salmon4
    fillet on top of the slices.
  3. Add a peeled, chopped, thumb-sized piece of ginger, a chopped chilli and a handful of chopped coriander stalks to the sheet. Add a small glass of water, and the same amount of soy sauce.
  4. Cover the sheet tightly in foil (you don't want any of the steam to escape).
  5. Bake in the oven for 12 minutes
  6. While the
    salmon4
    is in the oven, put the wild
    rice400 g
    (in the ratio of 1 part
    rice400 g
    to 2 parts boiling water) into a pan and boil until the water has been absorbed.
  7. Take the
    salmon4
    out of the oven. Remove the foil and scatter over some coriander leaves and an extra sliced chilli, if you want some extra kick.
  8. Serve the
    salmon4
    on top of the
    rice400 g
    with all the juices poured on top.
