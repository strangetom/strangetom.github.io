Curried lentil, tomato and coconut soup

Coriander stalks can all too often be thrown away but they shouldn't be: they have the texture of chives and taste, unsurprisingly, of coriander. Serve with lime wedges, if you like, for a citrusy kick.

40 mins
4 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp coconut oil (or sunflower oil)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped (160 g)
  • 1 tbsp medium curry powder
  • ½ tsp chilli flakes
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 4 cm piece of ginger, peeled and finely chopped (30 g)
  • 150 g red lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 1x 400 g tin of chopped tomatoes
  • 25 g coriander stalks, roughly chopped plus 5 g picked leaves to garnish
  • 1x 400 ml tin of coconut milk
  • salt and black pepper

Instructions

  1. Put the
    oil2 tbsp
    into a medium saucepan and place on a medium high heat. Add the
    onion1
    and fry for 8 minutes, stirring frequently, until soft and caramelised. Add the
    curry powder1 tbsp
    ,
    chilli flakes½ tsp
    ,
    garlic2 cloves
    and
    ginger4 cm
    and continue to fry for 2 minutes, stirring continuously. Add the
    lentils150 g
    , stir through for a minute, then add the
    tomatoes1x 400 g tin
    ,
    coriander stalks25 g
    , 600 ml of water, 1 teaspoon of salt and a very generous grind of pepper.
  2. Pour the
    coconut milk1x 400 ml tin
    into a bowl and gently whisk until smooth and creamy. Set aside 4 tablespoons - you'll need this when serving - and add all the remaining
    coconut milk1x 400 ml tin
    to the soup. Bring to the boil, then reduce the heat to medium and simmer gently for 25 minutes, until the
    lentils150 g
    are soft but still holding their shape. Add a little bit more water - about 100-150 ml - if your soup needs thinning down.
  3. Divide the soup between four bowls, drizzle over the reserved
    coconut milk1x 400 ml tin
    , sprinkle with coriander leaves and serve.
