Fettuccine with asparagus and smoked salmon

Fresh pasta, asparagus and smoked salmon are tossed with shallot cream sauce in this elegant weeknight dinner that can be prepared in well under an hour.

35 mins
4 servings

Ingredients

  • ½ lb fresh asparagus, medium thickness
  • salt
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • ½ tbsp minced shallots
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 4 oz smoked salmon sliced 0.25" thick
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp fresh lemon juice
  • 9-10 oz fresh green fettuccine noodles
  • 2 tbsp minced fresh dill

Instructions

  1. Snap off the ends of the
    asparagus½ lb
    where they break naturally and peel the stalks. Cut the
    asparagus½ lb
    on a slant to pieces about an inch long.
  2. Steam the
    asparagus½ lb
    until they are just barely tender and still bright green, about three minutes. Rinse under cold water, drain well on paper towels and set aside.
  3. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil for the pasta.
  4. While the water is coming to a boil, melt the
    butter1 tbsp
    in a large heavy skillet. Add the shallots and saute until soft but not brown. Stir in the
    cream1 cup
    and simmer about five minutes, until the
    cream1 cup
    has thickened somewhat.
  5. Cut the
    salmon4 oz
    into slivers, add it to the
    cream1 cup
    and remove the skillet from the heat. Season with pepper and
    lemon juice1 tsp
    . Add the
    asparagus½ lb
    .
  6. When the pot of water is boiling, add the
    fettuccine9-10 oz
    , stir it once or twice, then cook two to three minutes after the water has returned to a boil. Drain well.
  7. Briefly reheat the sauce. Transfer the
    fettuccine9-10 oz
    to a warm serving bowl, pour the sauce over it and toss. Sprinkle with
    dill2 tbsp
    and serve.
