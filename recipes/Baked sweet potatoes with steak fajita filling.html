Baked sweet potatoes with steak fajita filling

70 mins
4 servings

Gluten Free

  • kcal 578
  • fat 30 g
  • saturates 7 g
  • carbs 39 g
  • sugar 24 g
  • fibre 13 g
  • protein 32 g
  • salt 0.5 g

Ingredients

  • 4 sweet potatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, plus extra to rub on the potatoes
  • juice 1 lime
  • 2 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 500 g lean beef steak, cut into medium-sized strips
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • a mixture of 4 orange, yellow and red peppers, thinly sliced
  • small handful coriander, chopped
  • 2 avocados, halved, stoned and peeled

Instructions

  1. Heat oven to 200°C/Gas 7. Wash the
    sweet potatoes4
    , prick them with a fork all over, then rub with a little
    olive oil2 tbsp
    and season generously. Wrap each potato in tin foil and bake on a baking tray for 45-55 mins until soft.
  2. To make the marinade, put the lime juice,
    chilli powder2 tsp
    ,
    garlic powder1 tsp
    , cumin, 1 tbsp
    olive oil2 tbsp
    and a generous pinch of seasoning in a bowl. Mix everything together, then pour half the marinade into a second bowl. Add the steak to one of the bowls and mix well. Add the
    onions2
    and peppers to the other bowl and mix to ensure everything is evenly coated. Cover both bowls in cling film and chill in the fridge until needed.
  3. When the
    sweet potatoes4
    have about 20 mins left to cook, heat the remaining 1 tbsp
    olive oil2 tbsp
    in a large frying pan over a medium heat. Add the onion and pepper mixture, cook for 12-15 mins, stirring often, until they go soft and start to glisten and brown, then tip onto a plate. Wipe the pan clean with some kitchen paper, add the steak and cook for 3-5 mins, stirring often. Add the
    onions2
    and peppers back to the pan for a few mins before serving, then remove from the heat and stir through most of the coriander.
  4. Mash the avocado in a bowl with a fork. Add a good pinch of salt and the rest of the coriander.
  5. To serve, halve each sweet potato lengthways, mash the insides with a fork, then top with a spoonful of the steak fajita mixture and a dollop of mashed avocado. Enjoy!
