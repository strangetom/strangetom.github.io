Tomato pork bombs

Servings: 4

Total: 1 hr 10 mins

Ingredients

  • 50 g white basmati rice, rinsed and drained
  • 2 tbsp sunflower oil, plus extra for oiling
  • 2 small onions, chopped
  • 450 g minced pork
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • ¼ tsp dried marjoram
  • 1 tbsp chopped parsley leaves
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 2½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp butter, softened
  • 1½ tbsp plain flour
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 6 tbsp tomato puree/paste
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar

Instructions

  1. Put the
    rice50 g
    and 120 ml water in a sauce pan over a medium-high heat and bring to the boil. Turn the heat down to low and simmer for 5 minutes until the grains just begin soften so that they can be crushed between the fingers. Drain and rinse with cold water to stop the cooking process.
  2. Heat the
    oil2 tbsp
    in a frying pan and saute the
    onions2 small
    over a medium heat for about 5 minutes until soft.
  3. Mix the
    minced pork450 g
    ,
    rice50 g
    ,
    onions2 small
    ,
    egg1
    ,
    marjoram¼ tsp
    ,
    parsley1 tbsp
    ,
    paprika1 tsp
    , 0.5 tsp of the
    salt2½ tsp
    and the
    black pepper¼ tsp
    in a large mixing bowl.
  4. Fill a large sauce pan with water and add 2 tsp of
    salt2½ tsp
    to the water. Bring to the boil.
  5. With oiled hands, form meatballs about 4 cm in diameter. Drop the balls into the vigorously boiling water and cooking for 8-10 minutes until the balls become firm. Remove carefully and put on a tray, reserving the cooking liquid.
  6. Meanwhile, to make the sauce, melt the
    butter2 tbsp
    in a saucepan over a low heat. Sprinkle the
    flour1½ tbsp
    over the
    butter2 tbsp
    , stir and cook until the mixture foams up. Do not let it brown. Blend the
    cornflour1 tbsp
    to a paste with 2 tbsp water. Stir the
    tomato puree/paste6 tbsp
    and 950 ml of the water in which the meatballs were cooked into the sauce and bring to the boil, stirring continuously. As it comes to the boil, stir in the
    cornflour1 tbsp
    paste until the sauce thickens.
  7. Stir in the
    sugar2 tbsp
    ,
    salt2½ tsp
    and the
    red wine vinegar2 tbsp
    . Taste and adjust the seasoning with
    salt2½ tsp
    and pepper, if needed. Add more
    sugar2 tbsp
    or vinegar to suit your taste. You are looking for a sweet, tangy tomato base.
  8. Now carefully transfer the meatballs to the tomato sauce. Loosen with a touch of water to ensure that all the meatballs are covered with the tomato sauce. Simmer gently for 15 minutes until the meatballs are cooked through. Serve straight away.
