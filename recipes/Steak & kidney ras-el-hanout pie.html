Beef

325 mins

4 servings

Steak & kidney ras-el-hanout pie


Ingredients

  • 140 g lamb's kidney, chopped into small pieces
  • 200 ml whole milk
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 clove garlic, crushed
  • 5 cm fresh ginger, peeled and crushed
  • 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 400 g beef steak (e.g. braising steak), cut into small chunks
  • 300 g chestnut mushrooms, cut into small chunks
  • 4 tsp ras el hanout
  • 150 ml ginger beer
  • 3 spring onions, finely chopped
  • 350g self-raising flour
  • 75 g unsalted butter
  • 100 g suet
  • 150 ml cold water

Instructions

  1. Put the kidney in a bowl, pour in the milk, stir and set aside
  2. Heat the oil in a large non-stick pan on a medium heat. Add the garlic, ginger, onion and salt and cook for 5-10 minutes, until soft. If it starts to stick, don't be tempted to take the pan off the heat, just add a little water and help it come away
  3. Now add the steak and cook until the meat is brown. Add the mushrooms and cook for 10 minutes
  4. Drain the kidney and rinse under cold water. Add to the pan and cook for 10 minutes, then stir in the ras el hanout and cook for 5 more minutes before pouring in the ginger beer.
  5. Cook for 10-15 minutes more, until all reduced down. Take off the heat, stir in the spring onions and leave to cool completely.
  6. Preheat the oven to 170°C/Gas 5 and boil a full kettle of water. Grease a 23 x 13 cm loaf tin
  7. Start on the pastry by putting the flour, butter and suet into a bowl. Rub with your fingertips until it resembles breadcrumbs. Make a well in the centre, add the cold water and bring the dough together. Set a third of it aside.
  8. Roll the large piece of pastry to a rectangle of 40 x 30 cm, large enough to the line the tin with some overhang. Line the tin, leaving the excess pastry attached. Spoon in the cooled pie filling.
  9. Roll out the rest of the pastry and place in on top, cutting it to jst cover the filling. Trim the overhang, using the sides of the tin as a guide, and folder over the edges, working all the way around
  10. Place a lightly greased rectangle of baking paper over the top of the pie. Cover with foil, then sit the loaf tine in a large roasting tin. Pour boiling water into the roasting tin, to come halfway up the outside of the loaf tin, and steam in the over for 2 hours.
  11. Take out of the oven and transfer to a plate. Serve the pie with mashed potatoes.
