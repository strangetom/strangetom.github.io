All-in-one fish, chickpea & spinach stew

Serves 4     |     Prep 25 mins     |     Cook 55 mins

The foundations of this dish lie in Marcella Hazanâ€™s famous pasta sauce that calls for only four ingredients; tomatoes, butter, a whole onion and salt. While Hazan instructs to remove the onion, so it merely infuses the tomato sauce, I like a stronger onion flavour and so, in my recipe, the onion is grated, melting into the other ingredients over the course of time. This recipe is simple and clean, allowing the short list of ingredients to do all the talking.

Source: Foolproof One-Pot: 60 Simple and Satisfying Recipes

Ingredients

  • 75 g unsalted butter
  • 2x 400 g can chopped tomatoes
  • 200 g cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 onion, peeled and coarsely grated
  • 2 sprigs of thyme
  • 1 clove garlic, finely grated
  • pinch of chilli flakes
  • 8 raw, unshelled tiger prawns
  • 1x 400 g can chickpeas, drained
  • 125 g baby spinach
  • 400 g skinless white fish, cut into 4 cm (1.5 in) pieces, like cod loin
  • juice of 0.5-1 lemon, to taste
  • sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • crusty bread, to serve
