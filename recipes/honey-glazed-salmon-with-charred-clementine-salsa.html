Honey-glazed salmon with charred clementine salsa

30 mins Serves 2

The citrus in this wintery salso cuts through the richness of the salmon

Ingredients

  • 160 g basmati rice
  • 4 clementines, peeled and cut into rings
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • ½ red pepper, cut into 1 cm chunks
  • ½ small clove garlic, crushed
  • 0.5x 25 g pack coriander, finely chopped
  • juice of 0.5 lime
  • 1 red chilli, deseeded and finely chopped (optional)
  • 220 g salmon fillets
  • 1 tbsp clear honey
  • pinch smoked paprika
  • 200 g tenderstem brocolli, to serve

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C/Gas 7. Cook the basmati rice according to the pack instructions. Fluff with a fork and cover with a lid and set aside.
  2. Heat a nonstick frying pan over a high heat. When it starts to smoke, carefully arrange the clementine rings in one layer. Cook for 1-2 minutes on each side until charred, then chop.
  3. Put the clementine and any juices in a bowl and stir through half the oil, the pepper, garlic, coriander, lime juice and chilli (if using). Season then set aside.
  4. Line a baking tray with greaseproof paper, then place the salmon fillets on top. Drizzle with the honey and remaining oil, season, then sprinkle over the smoked paprika. Roast for 12-15 minutes until the fish is cooked through.
  5. Meanwhile, bring a pan of water to the boil, add the brocolli and cook for 3-5 minutes until tender. Drain well.
  6. Serve the salmon fillets on the rice with a spoonful of the clementine salsa and a few stems of brocolli per person.
