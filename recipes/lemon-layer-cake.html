Lemon layer cake

45 mins Serves 12

This light and lemony sponge sums up those first days of spring is prepared in minutes using the 'all in one' method.

Ingredients

  • 225 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 125 caster sugar
  • 100 g golden syrup
  • 225 g self-raising flour
  • Â½ tsp baking powder
  • 4 large eggs
  • finely grated zest of 1 lemon
  • 100 g unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp golden syrup
  • 200 g icing sugar
  • pared zest of 1 lemon, plus 3 tbsp of juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 160°C/Gas 4. Grease and base-line 2 x 20 cm loose based sandwich tins.
  2. Using an electric whisk, beat the butter, sugar, syrup, flour, baking powder, eggs and grated lemon zest together in a bowl until combined. Scrape down the sides using a spatula then whisk again until the mixture is pale and fluffy.
  3. Divide the batter equally between the 2 prepared cake tins and level the tops. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cakes are golden and springy to the touch. Cool in the tins for 5 minutes then loosen the edges with a knife and turn out onto wire racks. Remove and discard the base paper.
  4. For the icing, cream the unsalted butter and golden syrup in a large bowl until smooth and pale. Add the icing sugar and lemon juice, then beat until the mixture is light and fluffy.
  5. Put 1 sponge onto a stand or plate. Spread over half the icing, then top with the second sponge. Spread the remaining icing over the top and decorate with the pared lemon zest.
