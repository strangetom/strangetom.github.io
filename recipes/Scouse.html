Scouse

2 hr 25 mins Serves 4

This meat-and-potato stew from Liverpool has versions throughout northern Europe, and not even Scousers agree what the best recipe is.

Ingredients

  • 800 g scrag end/lamb neck, on the bone in thick slices or 600 g boneless lamb shoulder
  • 2 tbsp beef dripping or neutral oil
  • 500 g floury potatoes, cleaned
  • 2 onions
  • 600 ml beef stock
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 carrots
  • 250 g swede, optional
  • Salt and pepper
  • Worcestershire sauce, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the oven to 140°C/Gas 3; alternatively, cook this on the hob. Heat the fat in a large, lidded saucepan or ovenproof pan over a medium-high heat, then sear the
    meat800 g
    in batches, until properly browned.
  2. Lift out and set aside. Meanwhile, peel half the
    potatoes500 g
    and cut into small cubes, setting the rest aside for the time being, and peel and thinly slice the
    onions2
    .
  3. Turn down the heat, add the
    onions2
    and fry, stirring regularly, until soft.
  4. Now add the cubed
    potatoes500 g
    , fry for five minutes more, then stir in the
    stock600 ml
    , scraping the bits off the bottom of the pan as you do so, and return the
    meat800 g
    to the pot along with the herbs.
  5. Bring to a simmer, then cover and put in the oven (or leave on a low heat) for 60 minutes, until the
    potatoes500 g
    have begun to dissolve into the sauce. Mash a few of them against the sides of the pot to help them along.
  6. Meanwhile, peel and cube the
    carrots2
    and
    swede250 g
    , if using, and dice the remaining
    potatoes500 g
    – there’s no need to peel them.
  7. After the scouse has been cooking for 60 minutes, add the vegetables to the pot and return to the oven (or hob) for another hour, or until the vegetables are tender.
  8. Season to taste with salt, pepper and a dash of Worcestershire sauce, and serve hot with pickled cabbage or beetroot.
