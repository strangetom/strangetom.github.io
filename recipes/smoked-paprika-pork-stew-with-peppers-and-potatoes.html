Smoked paprika pork stew with peppers and potatoes

35 mins Serves 4

A goulash-style pork dish that makes a nice easy supper to serve to friends or family - just add crusty bread!

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2x 300 g pack extra lean pork medallions
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 red pepper, deseeded and diced
  • 1 green pepper, deseeded and diced
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp caraway seeds
  • Â½ tsp chilli powder
  • 250 g new potatoes, larger ones halved
  • 1x 400 g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato puree
  • chopped parsley
  • fat-free natural greek yogurt and bread, to serve

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a large frying pan over a high heat, then fry the pork medallions in batches for 3 minutes on each side until golden brown. Transfer to a plate and set aside
  2. Reduce the heat to medium, then fry the onion and peppers for 2-3 minutes in the remaining oil, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  3. Add the garlic, paprika, caraway seeds and chilli powder to the pan and cook, stirring, for 30 seconds. Stir in the potatoes, tomatoes, tomato puree and 150 ml water, and bring the mixture to the boil.
  4. Season, the return the pork to the pan, cover and simmer for 15 minutes until the potatoes are tender. Scatter with the parsley and serve with the bread and yogurt.
