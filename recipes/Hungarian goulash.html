Hungarian goulash

590 mins
4 servings

Ingredients

  • 4 tbsp sunflower oil
  • 650 g braising steak, cut into 2.5 cm cubes
  • 2 tsp plain flour
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 300 ml beef stock
  • 3 onions, chopped
  • 4 carrots, diced
  • 1 large potato, diced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp caraway seeds
  • 400 g can chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp soured cream
  • salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Heat half the oil in a heavy-based frying pan. Add the beef and cook over a medium heat, stirring frequently, until evenly browned. Reduce the heat and stir in the flour and
    paprika2 tsp
    . Cook, stirring constantly for 2 minutes. Gradually stir in the stock and bring to the boil, then transfer the mixture to the slow cooker.
  2. Wipe out the pan with kitchen paper, then heat the remaining oil. Add the
    onions3
    and cook over a low heat, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes, until softened. Stir in the
    carrots4
    and
    potato1 large
    and cook for a further few minutes. Add the
    bay leaf1
    and
    caraway seeds1 tsp
    and tomatoes. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  3. Transfer the vegetable mixture to the slow cooker and stir well, then cover and cook on low for 9 hours, until the meat is tender.
  4. Remove and discard the
    bay leaf1
    . Stir in the
    soured cream2 tbsp
    and transfer to warmed serving plates. Serve immediately.
