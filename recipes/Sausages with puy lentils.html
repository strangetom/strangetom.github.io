Sausages with puy lentils

Lentils have a great affinity with pork and sausages as they have the capacity to absorb the richness.

Servings: 4

Total: 1 hr 30 mins

Ingredients

  • 8 large good-quality, herby pork sausages
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and freshly milled black pepper
  • 175 g Puy lentils
  • 1 large onion, chopped small
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 25 g butter
  • 2 tbsp groundnut or other flavourless oil
  • 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh thyme

Instructions

  1. Begin by rinsing the
    lentils175 g
    , then place them in a saucepan with 1 litre of water (don't add any salt), bring them to simmering point and cook gently for about 30 minutes, or until the
    lentils175 g
    are tender but not disintegrating.
  2. Then drain them and keep the cooking liquid. Meanwhile, fry the
    onion1 large
    and
    garlic2 cloves
    in the
    butter25 g
    and 1 tablespoon of the
    oil2 tbsp
    in the casserole for 8-10 minutes to soften. Next, add the tinned
    tomatoes400 g
    ,
    sugar1 tsp
    ,
    thyme1 tbsp
    and
    bay leaf1
    , and some salt and freshly milled black pepper, and let it all simmer gently (without a lid) until the liquid reduces and it becomes rather thick - you may need to stir it now and then and it will take about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Now heat the remaining tablespoon of
    oil2 tbsp
    in a large frying pan and brown the
    sausages8 large
    all over in it. Then, using a draining spoon, transfer them to the tomato mixture. Add the cooked
    lentils175 g
    and 10 fl oz (275 ml) of the liquid they were cooked in. Stir well, then continue cooking over a very gentle heat for about 30 minutes - with a lid - and, if the mixture begins to look dry, add a little more liquid.
  4. Taste and add pepper and salt, if it needs it, before serving.
