The almighty mob chicken pie

The king of the pies. The tarragon makes it. Mandatory dollop of mustard. You have to give this one a go.

1 hr 0 mins
4 servings

Ingredients

  • 750 g boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 leeks
  • 1 brown onion
  • smoked bacon
  • 1 pint whole milk
  • bunch of tarragon
  • block puff pastry
  • plain flour
  • 1 egg
  • pot of dijon mustard

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Dice up your
    chicken750 g
    thighs into chunks. Brown them off in a large pan with some olive oil. Remove the
    chicken750 g
    from the pan and set aside.
  3. Finely slice your

    , bacon and

    .
  4. Add bacon to the
    chicken750 g
    pan, and fry until crispy. Deglaze the pan with a splash of water, and then add the
    leeks2
    and onion.
  5. Fry until soft, adding a splash of water if the vegetables start catching on the pan.
  6. Once soft, re-add the
    chicken750 g
    . Stir it in, and then add a large handful of chopped
    tarragonbunch
    . Stir, and then add 2 heaped tablespoons of flour.
  7. Mix it in, making sure the flour is absorbed by the mixture, and then pour in a pint of
    milk1 pint
    , splash by splash. Keep stirring until you have a thick pie filling. Season generously with pepper, and then remove the pan from the heat.
  8. Take a pie dish. Fill it with the filling. Then lay your puff pastry on top. Trim the sides, and brush the pastry with
    egg1
    yolk wash. Add a sprinkle of salt, and then place in the oven for 30 minutes, or until the pastry is puffed up and golden.
  9. Slice your pie up, and serve with a big dollop of Dijon mustard. Enjoy!
