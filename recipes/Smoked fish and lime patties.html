Smoked fish and lime patties

Servings: 2

Total: 45 mins

Ingredients

For the filling
  • 300 ml milk
  • 250 g smoked haddock
  • 50 g butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp plain white flour
  • ½ red chilli, deseeded
  • 70 g frozen peas
  • zest of 2 limes
For the patty pastry
  • 225 g plain flour
  • 1½ tsp ground turmeric
  • pinch of salt
  • 115 g cold butter, cut into small cubes
  • ½ egg, lighly beaten

Instructions

  1. Put the
    milk300 ml
    and
    haddock250 g
    in a large pan and bring to a simmer. Poach for 5 minutes.
  2. Remove the fish from the pan (keep the
    milk300 ml
    ) and flake, discarding any skin and bones.
  3. Melt the
    butter50 g
    in a frying pan and gently cook for
    onion1 large
    and
    garlic1 clove
    until soft. Sprinkle on the
    flour1 tbsp
    and stir for 1-2 minutes. Add the warm fish-poaching
    milk300 ml
    gradually, stirring after each addition to get a smooth sauce. Stir in the
    chilli½
    and
    peas70 g
    . Add the lime zest, then gently fold in the cooked fish and leave to cool completey.
  4. Put the
    flour1 tbsp
    ,
    turmeric1½ tsp
    and
    saltpinch
    in a bowl and rub in the
    cold butter115 g
    . Add 4-5 tbsp water and mix. Roll into a ball and chill for 30 minutes. Preheat the oven to 200°C/Gas 6.
  5. Cut the pastry into 3 or 4 pieces. Roll the first piece out into a large oval about 0.5 cm thick.
  6. Put a quarter of the filling in the middle, leaving about 4 cm around the edge. Brush the edge with water and fold the pastry lengthways. Use a fork to crimp the edges.
  7. Prick the patties with a fork, brush with the beaten
    egg½
    and cook in the oven for 25 minutes or until golden brown.
