Smoked fish and lime patties

  • Seafood

  • 45 mins

  • 2 servings

Ingredients

  • For the filling
  • 300 ml milk
  • 250 g smoked haddock
  • 50 g butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp plan white flour
  • 0.5 red chilli, deseeded
  • 70 g frozen peas
  • zest of 2 limes
  • For the patty pastry
  • 225 g plain flour
  • 1.5 tsp ground turmeric
  • pinch of salt
  • 115 g cold butter, cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 egg, lighly beaten

Instructions

  1. Put the milk and haddock in a large pan and bring to a simmer. Poach for 5 minutes.
  2. Remove the fish from the pan (keep the milk) and flake, discarding any skin and bones.
  3. Melt the butter in a frying pan and gently cook for onion and garlic until soft. Sprinkle on the flour and stir for 1-2 minutes. Add the warm fish-poaching milk gradually, stirring after each addition to get a smooth sauce. Stir in the chilli and peas. Add the lime zest, then gently fold in the cooked fish and leave to cool completey.
  4. Put the flour turmeric and salt in a bowl and rub in the butter. Add 4-5 tbsp water and mix. Roll into a ball and chill for 30 minutes. Preheat the oven to 200°C/Gas 6.
  5. Cut the pastry into 3 or 4 pieces. Roll the first piece out into a large oval about 0.5 cm thick.
  6. Put a quarter of the filling in the middle, leaving about 4 cm around the edge. Brush the edge with water and fold the pastry lengthways. Use a fork to crimp the edges.
  7. Prick the patties with a fork, brush with the beaten egg and cook in the oven for 25 minutes or until golden brown.
