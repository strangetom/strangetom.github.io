Beef curry

Make our easy beef curry and serve with a hunk of naan bread to mop up the delicious juices. If you prefer it less spicy, simply add less chilli powder.

Servings: 4

Total: 2 hr 50 mins

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 500 g diced braising steak
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 thumb sized piece ginger, finely grated
  • ¼ tsp hot chilli powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 3 pods cardamom, crushed
  • 400 g can chopped tomatoes
  • 300 ml beef stock
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp double cream (optional)
  • ½ small bunch coriander, roughly chopped
  • naan bread or rice, to serve

Instructions

  1. Heat one tbsp of the
    oil2 tbsp
    in a casserole pot over a medium-high heat. Season the
    beef500 g
    and fry in the pot for 5-8 mins, turning with a pair of tongs half way until evenly browned. Set aside on a plate.
  2. Heat the remaining
    oil2 tbsp
    and
    butter1 tbsp
    in the pan and add the onions. Fry gently for 15 mins or until golden brown and caramelised. Add the
    garlic2 cloves
    ,
    ginger1 thumb sized piece
    ,
    chilli¼ tsp
    ,
    turmeric1 tsp
    ,
    ground coriander2 tsp
    and
    cardamom3 pods
    and fry for two mins. Tip in the
    tomatoes400 g can
    ,
    stock300 ml
    and
    sugar1 tsp
    and bring to the simmer.
  3. Add the
    beef500 g
    , put a lid on top of the curry and cook over a low heat for 1½ - 2 hrs or until the meat is tender and falling apart. Remove the lid for the last 20 minutes of cooking.
  4. Stir through the
    garam masala2 tsp
    and
    cream2 tbsp
    (if using) and season to taste. Scatter over the
    coriander½ small bunch
    and serve with naan breads or rice.
