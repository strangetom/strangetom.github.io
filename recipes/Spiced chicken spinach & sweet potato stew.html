Spiced chicken, spinach & sweet potato stew

A healthy one-pot with warming spices that's low fat, low calorie, 3 of your 5-a-day and also full of fibre!

Servings: 4

Total: 55 mins

Ingredients

  • 3 sweet potatoes, cut into chunks
  • 190 g bag spinach
  • 1 tbsp sunflower oil
  • 8 chicken thighs, skinless and boneless
  • 500 ml chicken stock
For the spice paste
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 1 red chilli, chopped
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • thumb-sized piece ginger, grated
  • 400 g can tomatoes
  • 2 preserved lemons, deseeded and chopped
To serve
  • pumpkin seeds, toasted
  • 2-3 preserved lemons, deseeded and chopped
  • 4 naan bread, warmed

Instructions

  1. Put the
    sweet potato3
    in a large, deep saucepan over a high heat. Cover with boiling water and boil for 10 mins. Meanwhile, put all the paste ingredients in a food processor and blend until very finely chopped. Set aside until needed.
  2. Put the
    spinach190 g
    in a large colander in the sink and pour the sweet potatoes and their cooking water over it to drain the potatoes and wilt the
    spinach190 g
    at the same time. Leave to steam-dry.
  3. Return the saucepan to the heat (no need to wash it first), then add the
    oil1 tbsp
    , followed by the spice paste. Fry the paste for about 5 mins until thickened, then add the
    chicken8
    . Fry for 8-10 mins until the
    chicken8
    starts to colour. Pour over the
    stock500 ml
    , bring to the boil and leave to simmer for 10 mins, stirring occasionally.
  4. Check the
    chicken8
    is cooked by cutting into one of the thighs and making sure it's white throughout with no signs of pink. Season with black pepper, then add the
    sweet potato3
    . Leave to simmer for a further 5 mins. Meanwhile, roughly chop the
    spinach190 g
    and add to the stew. At this point you can leave the stew to cool and freeze for up to 3 months, if you like.
  5. Scatter over the pumpkin seeds and
    preserved lemons2
    , and serve with warm
    naan bread4
    on the side.
